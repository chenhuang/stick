To the Editor:Re "The Age ofBig Data" (news analysis, Sunday Review, Feb. 12) and "The Dubious Science of Online Dating," by Eli J. Finkel and Benjamin R. Karney (Sunday Review, Feb. 12):What a splendid juxtaposition: the relentless march of quantification and the inscrutable human heart!One can mine data until the cows come home, but strategic decisions, in both business and romance, are qualitative in nature. Such choices are a complex blend of informed intuition, feelings and interactions -- as well as data -- that cannot (yet) be captured by algorithms.ROBERT W. KEIDEL  Philadelphia, Feb. 13, 2012The writer, a clinical professor of management at Drexel University, is the author of "The Geometry of Strategy."

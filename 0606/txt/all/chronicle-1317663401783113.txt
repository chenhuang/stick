Udacity, a Silicon Valley start-up that creates online college classes, will announce on Tuesday a deal with San Jose State University for a series of remedial and introductory courses,The New York Timesreported. Courses in the pilot program—which will include remedial algebra, college-level algebra, and introductory statistics—will be limited 300 students. The cost of each three-unit course will be $150, significantly less than regular San Jose State tuition.
Source:NytimesVia:The New York TimesThe state university’s deal with Udacity is the first time that professors at a university have collaborated with a provider of a MOOC—massively open online course—to create courses with students first watching videos on their own, with support from online mentors, and then coming to class to work on assignments with a professor. Eventually, such blended courses could be offered to hundreds of thousands of students in the state.
